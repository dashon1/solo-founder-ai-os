# The Solo Founder Task Routing System
## A Decision Framework for What to Do Yourself, Delegate to AI, Delegate to a Human, or Eliminate

Every hour you spend on the wrong task is an hour you're not spending on the work that actually grows your business. The challenge for solo founders is that there are no guardrails — no manager to tell you what matters, no team to absorb the low-value work. It's all on you to make routing decisions constantly.

This system gives you a repeatable decision framework so you stop defaulting to "I'll just do it myself" and start asking the right question: *What is the highest-leverage use of the next 60 minutes?*

---

## The Four Routing Categories

Every task you face falls into one of four buckets:

### 1. DO IT YOURSELF
Tasks that require your unique expertise, judgment, lived experience, or relationships. Things only you can do, or things where your personal involvement is part of the value.

**Signs a task belongs here:**
- It requires your authentic voice or personal perspective
- The relationship or trust depends on your involvement
- A decision that will define your company's direction
- Creative or strategic work where your vision is the product

**Examples:** Investor conversations, high-stakes sales calls, core product decisions, content where your personal story is the differentiation.

---

### 2. DELEGATE TO AI
Tasks that are structured, repeatable, and don't require real-world relationships or original judgment. AI excels at synthesis, drafting, analysis, and generating options.

**Signs a task belongs here:**
- It starts with "write a first draft of..."
- It starts with "summarize..." or "research..."
- It involves formatting, categorizing, or reorganizing information
- It's a task you've done the same way 10+ times before

**Examples:** First drafts of emails, research summaries, data analysis, template creation, copy variants, transcription, FAQ answers.

---

### 3. DELEGATE TO A HUMAN
Tasks that require real-world action, judgment under ambiguity, or human relationship management — but not specifically *your* judgment or relationships.

**Signs a task belongs here:**
- It takes 3+ hours per week and is process-driven
- It requires coordination with other humans (scheduling, vendor calls)
- It involves technical skills you lack and don't want to build
- You've already done it enough times that an SOP exists or could be written

**Examples:** Bookkeeping, customer support tier 1, graphic design, appointment scheduling, paid ad management, video editing.

---

### 4. ELIMINATE
Tasks that feel necessary but produce no measurable business outcome. Usually disguised as "staying on top of things."

**Signs a task should be eliminated:**
- You can't name the business outcome it produces
- You do it out of habit or anxiety, not strategy
- Nothing bad would happen if you stopped for 30 days

**Examples:** Checking social media analytics daily, reading every newsletter you subscribed to, attending networking events with no clear lead potential, reformatting documents no one reads.

---

## The Routing Matrix

Use this 2-question decision tree for any task:

**Question 1:** Does this task require my specific judgment, voice, or relationship?  
**Question 2:** Does this task have a clear business outcome?

| Needs Your Judgment | Has Business Outcome | Route To |
|---|---|---|
| Yes | Yes | DO IT YOURSELF |
| No | Yes | AI or Human |
| Yes | No | Reconsider — can you reshape it to create outcome? |
| No | No | ELIMINATE |

For tasks landing in "AI or Human," ask one more question: **Does this require real-world action or human relationships?**
- If yes → Delegate to Human
- If no → Delegate to AI

## 20 Example Tasks, Routed and Explained

### Content and Marketing

**1. Writing a blog post about your methodology**
*Route: DO IT YOURSELF (first pass) → AI (drafting/editing)*
Your methodology is your intellectual property. AI can draft and refine, but the ideas and positioning must come from you. Hybrid: dictate your core ideas, let AI structure them.

**2. Writing promotional emails for a product launch**
*Route: DELEGATE TO AI*
Standard copywriting structure with your product info as input. Use AI to generate 3 variants, then pick and lightly personalize. Saves 2–4 hours per launch.

**3. Creating social media graphics**
*Route: DELEGATE TO HUMAN (or tool)*
Canva automation or a $15/hour designer on Fiverr handles this better and faster than you spending 90 minutes in design tools.

**4. Scheduling and publishing content**
*Route: DELEGATE TO AI/TOOL*
Buffer, Hypefury, or Publer — this is a zero-judgment automation task. Never do this manually.

**5. Responding to podcast interview requests**
*Route: DO IT YOURSELF*
Relationships and voice selection require your judgment. AI can draft the response, but you send it from you.

---

### Sales and Customer Success

**6. Writing cold outreach sequences**
*Route: DELEGATE TO AI*
Give AI the prospect's background, your product, and the desired CTA. Generate sequences in minutes.

**7. Taking a first call with a high-value prospect**
*Route: DO IT YOURSELF*
No exception. First calls with strategic or high-value prospects require you. The relationship is built here.

**8. Following up with stalled deals**
*Route: DELEGATE TO AI (draft) → DO IT YOURSELF (send)*
AI generates the follow-up message. You review, personalize the first line, and send. 15 minutes replaces 2 hours.

**9. Answering tier-1 customer support questions**
*Route: DELEGATE TO AI*
Build a response library and train a custom GPT or Intercom bot on your policies. Handle only escalations yourself.

**10. Resolving a complex customer complaint involving a refund**
*Route: DO IT YOURSELF*
High stakes + relationship = your involvement. AI can help you draft the response, but you own the resolution.

---

### Operations and Admin

**11. Bookkeeping and expense categorization**
*Route: DELEGATE TO HUMAN + TOOL*
QuickBooks with a part-time bookkeeper ($200–$500/month) handles this. Your time is worth more.

**12. Reviewing and analyzing your monthly P&L**
*Route: DELEGATE TO AI (analysis) → DO IT YOURSELF (decisions)*
Paste your P&L into Claude or ChatGPT for analysis. The decisions that follow are yours.

**13. Scheduling meetings and managing calendar blocks**
*Route: DELEGATE TO TOOL*
Calendly. Non-negotiable. Manual scheduling is time theft.

**14. Writing your quarterly investor update**
*Route: DO IT YOURSELF (key points) → AI (drafting)*
Investors are reading between the lines of how you think. AI can structure the narrative, but your honest assessment of the business must drive it.

**15. Reformatting a proposal template for a new client**
*Route: DELEGATE TO AI*
Give AI your existing template and the new client's context. It handles the reformatting in 5 minutes.

---

### Product and Development

**16. Defining a new feature from user research**
*Route: DO IT YOURSELF (decision) → AI (synthesis)*
Feed AI the raw user feedback and let it identify patterns. But the final feature definition is your call.

**17. Writing technical documentation**
*Route: DELEGATE TO AI*
Give AI your code comments, architecture notes, or a verbal explanation. Let it write the docs. You review.

**18. Bug triage and prioritization**
*Route: DO IT YOURSELF (priority call) → AI (research)*
AI can help diagnose and find solutions. You decide what gets fixed in this sprint.

**19. Competitive feature analysis**
*Route: DELEGATE TO AI*
Structured research task. Perplexity or ChatGPT with Browse can produce a thorough competitor feature matrix in 15 minutes.

**20. Deciding whether to pivot or persist**
*Route: DO IT YOURSELF (with AI as thought partner)*
This is a strategic inflection point. AI can stress-test your reasoning, but the call is yours alone. See Document 07 for the decision log framework.

## Implementing the System: Your Weekly Routing Audit

Once per week (Friday or Sunday), run this prompt against your task list:

```
Here is my task list for next week: [paste your list]
Apply the solo founder task routing framework:
- DO IT YOURSELF: requires my specific judgment, voice, or relationship
- DELEGATE TO AI: structured, repeatable, does not require real-world action
- DELEGATE TO HUMAN: requires real-world action or human relationships but not specifically mine
- ELIMINATE: no clear business outcome

Categorize each task, and flag any tasks I might be overcomplicating or doing myself when AI could handle it.
```

This audit, done consistently, is where founders reclaim 5–10 hours per week within 30 days.

---

## The Key Insight: Your Time Is the Constraint

You have approximately 40–50 productive hours per week. Every task you route incorrectly costs you not just that task's time, but the compound opportunity cost of what you could have done instead.

The founders who scale aren't always the smartest or the hardest-working. They're the ones who got ruthless about routing — protecting their highest-leverage hours and systematically eliminating, automating, and delegating everything else.

Use this framework. Revisit it monthly. Your routing instincts will get sharper over time.
