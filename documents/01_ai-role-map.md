# The Solo Founder AI Role Map
## Every Function of Your Business, Mapped to the Right AI Tool

As a solo founder, you are the CEO, marketer, sales rep, product manager, customer support agent, bookkeeper, and legal reviewer — all at once. The good news: each of those roles can now be augmented or nearly replaced by AI. The bad news: most founders use AI randomly rather than systematically.

This document gives you a complete map. For every major function in your business, you'll find the best AI tool, the highest-leverage use case, and a ready-to-use starter prompt. Read it once, then use it as a reference every time you sit down to work.

---

## 1. Content Creation

**Primary Tool:** ChatGPT (GPT-4o) or Claude Sonnet  
**Secondary:** Jasper, Copy.ai, Notion AI

**Best Use Case:** Drafting long-form content, rewriting for tone, building a content calendar, repurposing one piece into ten formats.

AI is not a ghostwriter — it's a first-draft machine. Your job is to add your perspective, real data, and lived experience on top of what it generates.

**Starter Prompt:**
```
You are a content strategist helping a solo founder in [your industry]. 
I need a 1,200-word blog post on the topic: "[your topic]". 
Audience: [describe your ideal customer]. 
Tone: Direct, practical, zero fluff. 
Include: A strong hook, 4 actionable sections with subheadings, and a CTA at the end. 
Do not use buzzwords. Write like an expert practitioner, not a generalist.
```

**Pro Tip:** After the first draft, ask: "Now rewrite the intro to be more provocative — start with a surprising statistic or counterintuitive statement."

## 2. Customer Support

**Primary Tool:** ChatGPT with a custom system prompt, or Intercom's Fin AI  
**Secondary:** Tidio AI, Freshdesk Freddy

**Best Use Case:** Drafting templated responses, building an FAQ knowledge base, escalation classification, writing email responses in seconds.

You do not need a $50K/year support hire to handle 80% of support queries. You need a trained AI and a good prompt library.

**Starter Prompt:**
```
You are a customer support specialist for [your product name], a [brief description]. 
A customer just sent this message: "[paste message]"
Draft a friendly, helpful, on-brand response. 
Our tone is: [warm/professional/casual]. 
If the issue requires a refund or escalation, flag it clearly at the top of your response.
```

**Pro Tip:** Build a "Support Bible" doc with your policies, FAQs, and tone guidelines. Paste it into any AI as a system prompt. Response quality goes up 3x.

## 3. Research

**Primary Tool:** Perplexity AI (for live web research) or Claude with documents  
**Secondary:** ChatGPT with Browse, Gemini Deep Research

**Best Use Case:** Market research, competitive analysis, trend identification, summarizing long documents, synthesizing customer feedback.

Perplexity is your best friend for anything requiring current data with citations. Claude is better for synthesizing documents you upload.

**Starter Prompt:**
```
I'm a founder in [industry]. Research the top 5 competitors to [your product/service].
For each competitor, provide:
- Pricing model
- Key value proposition
- Main weaknesses (look for negative reviews on G2, Reddit, or App Store)
- Who they target vs. who they miss
Format your response as a comparison table, then give me a 3-sentence strategic summary.
```

**Pro Tip:** Feed this research output directly into a pricing or positioning prompt. Chaining AI outputs is where real leverage comes from.

## 4. Finance Tracking

**Primary Tool:** ChatGPT or Claude (analysis layer) + QuickBooks/Wave (data layer)  
**Secondary:** Digits, Fathom

**Best Use Case:** Categorizing expenses, building financial models, interpreting your P&L, writing investor updates, forecasting cash flow with scenario analysis.

AI cannot replace accounting software for bookkeeping. But it can make the financial data you already have 10x more actionable.

**Starter Prompt:**
```
Here is my business P&L for the last 3 months: [paste data]
Act as a CFO reviewing this for the first time.
Identify:
1. The top 3 cost levers I should examine
2. Revenue trends — is growth accelerating, plateauing, or declining?
3. One scenario: if I cut [expense category] by 30%, what does that do to my margin?
Give me your honest assessment, not a diplomatic one.
```

**Pro Tip:** Run this monthly. Paste it into a decision log. In 6 months you'll have a financial narrative of your business that no accounting app can produce.

## 5. Sales Outreach

**Primary Tool:** ChatGPT or Claude for copy, Apollo.io or Clay for prospecting  
**Secondary:** Instantly AI, Smartlead

**Best Use Case:** Writing cold email sequences, personalizing outreach at scale, drafting follow-up messages, creating a pipeline cadence.

The biggest mistake founders make is writing generic outreach. AI's superpower here is personalization — feed it a LinkedIn profile or company page and get a hyper-relevant opener.

**Starter Prompt:**
```
You are a B2B sales expert. I'm reaching out to [prospect name/role] at [company].
Context about them: [paste 3–5 lines from their LinkedIn or company website]
My product: [1-sentence description]
Write a 4-line cold email with:
- A first line that references something specific about them
- A clear value proposition tied to a problem they likely have
- A low-friction CTA (asking for a 15-minute call or a yes/no reply)
No buzzwords. No "I hope this finds you well." Sound like a real person.
```

**Pro Tip:** Write 3 versions for the same prospect — test them in batches of 30 emails each. Let the data tell you which approach converts.

## 6. Legal and Compliance

**Primary Tool:** Claude (for document drafting/review) or Harvey AI  
**Secondary:** DoNotPay, Spellbook, Ironclad AI

**Best Use Case:** First-draft contract review, terms of service drafting, privacy policy generation, understanding regulatory requirements, employment agreements.

**Critical caveat:** AI legal output is a starting point, not a final product. For anything over $5K in potential liability, have a real attorney review the final version. That said, AI can get you 80% of the way there for a fraction of the cost.

**Starter Prompt:**
```
Review the following contract clause as if you were a lawyer protecting my interests as a small business owner: 
[paste clause]
Identify:
1. Any language that is one-sided or unusually favorable to the other party
2. Missing protections I should ask for
3. Any terms I should push back on during negotiation
Be direct and flag the highest-risk items first.
```

**Pro Tip:** Keep a "Contracts Learned" document. Whenever AI flags a risky clause, note it. You'll become a sharper contract reader over time.

## 7. Product Development

**Primary Tool:** Claude or GPT-4o for specs, GitHub Copilot or Cursor for code  
**Secondary:** Notion AI for documentation, Maze AI for user research synthesis

**Best Use Case:** Writing product requirement documents (PRDs), synthesizing user feedback into features, naming features, building roadmaps, writing technical specs.

**Starter Prompt:**
```
I'm a solo founder building [describe product]. 
I've collected the following user feedback: [paste feedback]
Act as a senior product manager. 
Synthesize this feedback and output:
1. The top 3 user problems I need to solve (ranked by frequency and severity)
2. A suggested feature or improvement for each problem
3. Effort level for each (Low / Medium / High)
4. Which one I should ship first and why
```

**Pro Tip:** Run every new feature idea through this prompt: "Play devil's advocate. Give me 5 reasons this feature might not actually solve the user's problem." This alone will save you months of wasted dev time.

## 8. Strategy and Decision-Making

**Primary Tool:** Claude (best for nuanced reasoning and long context)  
**Secondary:** ChatGPT o1 or o3 (best for structured analytical thinking)

**Best Use Case:** Working through major decisions, stress-testing your business model, exploring pivots, preparing for investor conversations, annual planning.

**Starter Prompt:**
```
I run [describe business]. Here is my current situation: [2–3 paragraphs]
I'm facing a decision: [describe the decision]
Options I'm considering:
1. [Option A]
2. [Option B]
3. [Option C]

Act as a board advisor who has seen 100+ startups. 
For each option, give me: upside, downside, risks I'm probably not seeing, and which one you'd pick given the information I've provided. Be direct.
```

## How to Use This Map

1. **Pick one function per week** to systematically replace with AI. Don't try to do everything at once.
2. **Save every prompt that works** in a personal prompt library (use Notion, a Google Doc, or even a text file).
3. **Iterate the prompts.** The starter prompts above are a baseline. Your context, industry, and voice will make them better over time.
4. **Track the time saved.** Log how long each task takes before and after AI. The numbers will motivate you to go deeper.

The solo founders who win in the next five years won't be the ones who work the hardest. They'll be the ones who use leverage most intelligently — and AI is the highest-ROI leverage available today.
