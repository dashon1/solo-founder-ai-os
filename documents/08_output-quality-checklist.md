# The AI Output Quality Checklist
## Before You Publish, Send, or Ship Anything Generated with AI

AI output is fast. It's often impressive on first read. And it can still be subtly wrong in ways that embarrass you, damage your reputation, or cost you business. The problem isn't that AI is bad — the problem is that founders skip the review step because the output feels finished.

Nothing from AI should be published, sent, or used directly without a quality pass. This checklist is that pass. Ten points. Five minutes. Non-negotiable.

---

## Why This Checklist Matters

Three categories of AI failure that happen to founders regularly:

**1. Factual errors.** AI confidently states incorrect statistics, misquotes research, or references things that don't exist. If you publish these, you've staked your credibility on a hallucination.

**2. Brand voice mismatch.** AI defaults to a generic, slightly corporate tone. If your brand is irreverent, personal, or technical, raw AI output will often sound like it was written by someone else — because it was.

**3. Legal and reputational risk.** AI sometimes generates claims you can't back up, makes comparisons to competitors that could create liability, or includes phrasing that doesn't match your jurisdiction's compliance requirements.

The checklist below catches all three.

---

## The 10-Point Pre-Publish Quality Checklist

Run this before publishing a blog post, sending a sales email, posting to social, submitting a proposal, or sharing any AI-generated content publicly.

---

### CHECK 1: Factual Accuracy

**Review:** Has AI included any specific statistics, studies, dates, names, or citations?

For every factual claim in the output:
- [ ] Can I verify this independently (quick Google or source check)?
- [ ] Did I confirm that quotes attributed to specific people are accurate?
- [ ] Did I verify that any referenced studies, reports, or data actually exist?

**Action if fail:** Remove the unverified claim entirely, or replace it with a verified stat. Do not soften it with "reportedly" — just verify or cut.

---

### CHECK 2: Brand Voice and Tone

**Review:** Does this sound like me?

Read the piece out loud. Then ask:
- [ ] Would a regular reader of my content recognize this as my voice?
- [ ] Have I removed generic AI phrases like "In today's fast-paced world," "It's no secret that," or "In conclusion"?
- [ ] Does the vocabulary and sentence length match how I normally write?
- [ ] Are there any phrases that feel uncharacteristically formal, corporate, or motivational-poster-y?

**Action if fail:** Rewrite the flagged sections in your actual voice. Even rewriting 20% of an AI draft can transform it from "generic AI content" to "clearly written by this person."

---

### CHECK 3: Personalization and Specificity

**Review:** Is this specific enough to be credible?

- [ ] Does the content include specific details, real examples, or concrete data (not just AI-generated placeholders)?
- [ ] If this is a sales email, does the first line reference something specific about the recipient — not just their company name?
- [ ] If this is a blog post, are the examples real, or are they vague fictional scenarios the AI invented?

**Action if fail:** Add at least one specific, real detail that AI could not have generated. A real customer name (with permission), a specific number from your own experience, or a personal anecdote. This is what makes your content worth reading.

---

### CHECK 4: Legal and Liability Risk

**Review:** Does anything in this content create legal exposure?

- [ ] Are there any claims about your product's results that you can't substantiate? ("Our users increase revenue by X%" — do you have evidence?)
- [ ] Are there any direct comparisons to competitors that could be seen as defamatory or misleading?
- [ ] Does this content comply with relevant regulations for your industry? (Healthcare, finance, legal, and education content have specific rules)
- [ ] If there's a guarantee or promise, is it actually what you offer?

**Action if fail:** Soften unsubstantiated claims to language you can defend ("customers report…" vs. "guaranteed to…"). Remove competitor comparisons that lack documented support. If in doubt, consult a lawyer or remove the claim.

---

### CHECK 5: Audience Fit

**Review:** Is this written for the right person?

- [ ] Does the language match the sophistication level of your audience? (Beginner-friendly vs. expert-to-expert)
- [ ] Does the content address a real problem your audience actually has?
- [ ] Would your ideal customer recognize themselves in the examples or scenarios used?

**Action if fail:** Replace AI-generated "generic audience" assumptions with details specific to your actual customers. If you've done customer interviews, pull real quotes and language from them.

---

### CHECK 6: Accuracy of Your Own Business Details

**Review:** If AI referenced your product, services, pricing, or policies — is it correct?

- [ ] Are all product names, features, and pricing accurate?
- [ ] Are URLs, email addresses, and contact information correct?
- [ ] Are any policy statements (refunds, timelines, guarantees) accurate as of today?

**Action if fail:** Update or remove any outdated or incorrect business details. This seems obvious, but AI sometimes invents plausible-sounding product details when given vague instructions.

---

### CHECK 7: Readability and Flow

**Review:** Is this actually a pleasure to read?

- [ ] Does each paragraph have a clear point?
- [ ] Is the structure logical — does one idea lead to the next?
- [ ] Are sentences under 25 words on average?
- [ ] Have you eliminated any redundant paragraphs that repeat earlier points?

**Action if fail:** Cut aggressively. Most first-draft AI content is 20–30% longer than it needs to be. Less is almost always more.

---

### CHECK 8: CTA Clarity

**Review:** Is there one clear action you want the reader to take?

- [ ] Is the call to action specific and singular? (Not "check out my website and follow me and sign up for my newsletter")
- [ ] Is it appropriate for the platform and context?
- [ ] Is the CTA easy to complete — no friction?

**Action if fail:** Pick one CTA. Make it clear. If you're not sure what the CTA should be, that's a strategy problem — solve it before publishing.

---

### CHECK 9: Originality and Over-Reliance on AI Patterns

**Review:** Does this read like it came from a template?

- [ ] Does the structure feel fresh, or does it follow the exact same format as every AI-generated piece in your industry?
- [ ] Have you added at least one original thought, perspective, or data point that AI could not have generated?
- [ ] If this is an opinion piece, is the actual opinion yours — and does it say something specific enough to be disagreed with?

**Action if fail:** Add your actual opinion. The most shareable content online is specific, contrarian, or surprising. Generic AI content is none of those things. Your job is to make it at least one.

---

### CHECK 10: Final Read-Aloud Test

**Review:** How does this sound when spoken?

Read the entire piece out loud before publishing. If you stumble on a sentence, your reader will stumble on it too. If it sounds robotic, it will read robotic.

- [ ] Passed read-aloud — flows naturally and sounds human
- [ ] Made edits based on read-aloud
- [ ] Ready to publish/send

---

## The Quick Version: 60-Second Sanity Check

When you're in a hurry and need a rapid pass (for short-form content like social posts or quick emails), run just these four:

1. **True?** — Can I verify every factual claim?
2. **Mine?** — Does this sound like me?
3. **Legal?** — Does anything here create liability?
4. **Worth it?** — Would I be comfortable if 10,000 people read this?

If all four are yes, send it. If any one is no, fix it first.

---

## Closing Principle

AI is a force multiplier — it makes the fast faster and the good better. But it doesn't replace editorial judgment. The founders who build real reputations with AI are the ones who use it as a drafting engine and then apply their own expertise, voice, and values to the final product.

The 5 minutes you spend on this checklist protect the reputation it took you years to build.
