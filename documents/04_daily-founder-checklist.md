# The Daily Founder Checklist
## A 15-Minute Morning AI Sync, Decision Log, and Evening Review System

Consistency beats intensity. The founders who build the most aren't the ones who have occasional 14-hour grind days — they're the ones who execute the same high-leverage routines every single day. This checklist gives you three anchors: a morning sync to prime your day, a decision log for real-time clarity, and an evening review to close the loop.

Total time investment: 20–30 minutes per day. ROI: enormous.

---

## PART 1: The 15-Minute Morning AI Sync

Run this every weekday before you open email, social media, or Slack. The goal is to start the day with context and intent, not reaction.

**When:** First 15 minutes of work (after coffee, before anything else)  
**Tool:** Claude, ChatGPT, or your AI of choice

---

### Step 1 — Yesterday's Carryover (2 minutes)

Before you open AI, spend 60 seconds writing down:
- What you did accomplish yesterday
- What you didn't finish
- Anything that's been bothering you mentally

This brain dump clears working memory and prevents yesterday's incomplete tasks from hijacking today.

---

### Step 2 — The Morning Context Prompt (5 minutes)

Paste this into AI every morning (customize once, reuse daily):

```
Good morning. I'm a solo founder running [your business description].
Today is [day of week].

Yesterday I: [paste your brief summary]
Today my planned Big Three are:
1. [priority 1]
2. [priority 2]  
3. [priority 3]

Here are any blockers or concerns on my mind: [paste]

Give me:
1. A quick gut-check — does my Big Three for today make sense given what I told you?
2. Is there anything I'm missing or avoiding that should be on this list?
3. One piece of tactical advice for tackling my #1 priority today
4. If I only complete ONE thing today, which of these three should it be and why?
```

This prompt forces you to articulate your priorities out loud (in text) and gets a second-opinion sanity check before you dive in. You'll be surprised how often the AI identifies something you're dodging.

---

### Step 3 — The One Sentence Intention (2 minutes)

After the AI response, write one sentence in your journal, notes app, or on a sticky note:

> "Today is a win if I [specific outcome]."

Not a task. An outcome. "Today is a win if I send the product demo to 5 prospects" — not "do sales."

Pin it where you can see it. Check back at noon.

---

### Step 4 — Priority Block Protection (3 minutes)

Check your calendar. Are your top priorities blocked? If a meeting snuck onto your morning, decide now whether to:
- Keep it and shift your deep work block to afternoon
- Reschedule it (most meetings can move)
- Decline it entirely

Do not start the day with an unprotected calendar and hope for the best.

---

### Morning Checklist (15 minutes total)

```
MORNING SYNC CHECKLIST — [Date]

[ ] Brain dump from yesterday complete (2 min)
[ ] Morning context prompt run and reviewed (5 min)
[ ] One-sentence win statement written and visible (2 min)
[ ] Calendar checked — priority blocks protected (3 min)
[ ] Email/Slack NOT opened yet until block 1 complete (3 min)

TODAY'S WIN STATEMENT:
"Today is a win if I ________________________________"

BIG THREE:
1. ________________________________________________
2. ________________________________________________
3. ________________________________________________

BIGGEST BLOCKER RIGHT NOW:
________________________________________________
```

## PART 2: The Daily Decision Log

Solo founders make dozens of decisions every day. Most of them vanish — you decide, move on, and have no record of what you decided or why. Weeks later, you revisit the same territory and make the same decision again. Or worse, you make the opposite decision without realizing you've contradicted yourself.

The decision log fixes this in under 5 minutes per entry.

**When to log:** Any time you make a decision with consequences that last more than one week.

---

### Decision Log Template

```
DECISION LOG ENTRY

Date: _______________
Decision: [One clear sentence describing the decision made]

Context: [What situation prompted this decision? What information was available?]

Options I considered:
1. _______________________________________________
2. _______________________________________________
3. _______________________________________________

AI input used: [Yes / No — if yes, paste the key output in 1–3 sentences]

Final decision: _______________________________________________

My reasoning: [Why this option over the others?]

Expected outcome: [What do I expect to happen in 30/60/90 days as a result?]

Review date: _______________  (set a calendar reminder)

---
[Leave space below for the OUTCOME review]

OUTCOME (fill in on review date):
What actually happened: _______________________________________________
Was the decision right? [Yes / No / Partially]
What I'd do differently: _______________________________________________
```

---

### How to Use AI as a Decision Thought Partner

Before logging a major decision, run this prompt:

```
I need to make a decision: [describe it]

Here's the full context: [explain the situation]

Options I'm considering:
1. [Option A]
2. [Option B]
3. [Option C]

Challenge my thinking. What assumptions am I making that might be wrong?
What's the downside scenario I'm probably not fully weighing?
What would a seasoned operator who has seen this situation 20 times before say?
What's the question I should be asking that I haven't asked yet?
```

Use the AI's response to sharpen your thinking, then log the final decision in the template above. The goal is not to have AI decide for you — it's to make sure you've stress-tested the reasoning before you commit.

## PART 3: The Evening Review Checklist

The evening review takes 10 minutes. It's the difference between a business that compounds and one that plateaus. When you review daily, you catch problems early, celebrate real wins, and build momentum that doesn't depend on motivation.

**When:** Last 10 minutes of your workday, before you close the laptop.

---

### Evening Review Template

```
EVENING REVIEW — [Date]

1. WIN STATEMENT CHECK
My win statement was: _______________________________________________
Did I achieve it? [ ] Yes  [ ] Partially  [ ] No

If no — what got in the way?
_______________________________________________

2. BIG THREE PROGRESS
[ ] Priority 1: [paste from morning] — Status: Done / In Progress / Blocked
[ ] Priority 2: [paste from morning] — Status: Done / In Progress / Blocked
[ ] Priority 3: [paste from morning] — Status: Done / In Progress / Blocked

3. ENERGY AUDIT
Where did I feel most focused and effective today?
_______________________________________________

Where did I lose time or focus?
_______________________________________________

One thing I would change about how I structured today:
_______________________________________________

4. TOMORROW SETUP
Top priority for tomorrow morning (before email):
_______________________________________________

Any blockers I need to resolve tonight or early tomorrow:
_______________________________________________

One thing I can prepare now to make tomorrow faster:
_______________________________________________

5. GRATITUDE / MOMENTUM NOTE (optional but recommended)
One thing that went well today that I want to remember:
_______________________________________________
```

---

### The Evening AI Debrief Prompt

Once per week (recommended: Friday evening), run this deeper debrief:

```
I'm doing my daily review. Here's my data:
- I planned to: [Big Three from morning]
- I actually completed: [what got done]
- I got stuck on: [where I lost momentum]
- Time-wasters today: [what stole time]

Give me honest feedback:
1. What pattern do you see in what I'm completing vs. avoiding?
2. Was the way I structured my day this week effective for my stated priorities?
3. What's one concrete change I should make to my daily routine next week?
```

## Making the System Stick

The first week feels mechanical. The second week feels natural. By week four, it's your operating system and you'll notice how disoriented you feel without it.

Two rules for making it stick:

**Rule 1: Imperfect is better than skipped.** If you only have 5 minutes, run the morning checklist and skip everything else. Consistency of the anchor habit is more valuable than perfection.

**Rule 2: Keep your templates in one place.** Create a "Daily Ops" folder in Notion, a recurring note in Apple Notes, or a pinned doc in Google Drive. Copying from a template every day adds friction. The easier you make it, the more often you'll do it.
