# The Solo Founder Weekly Planning System
## A Day-by-Day AI-Powered Framework for Running Your Business Like a Pro

Most solo founders run their week reactively — responding to whatever shows up in the inbox, context-switching every 30 minutes, and ending Friday wondering where the week went. This system fixes that.

The framework assigns each day of the week a specific function. You batch like work together, you use AI at the start of each day to load context fast, and you end Friday knowing exactly what moved the needle and what's coming next.

This is not a productivity hack. It's an operating system.

---

## Before You Start: The Sunday Setup (15 minutes)

Before Monday, spend 15 minutes doing a "brain dump" into AI. This primes your week and removes the cognitive weight of trying to hold everything in your head.

**Sunday Brain Dump Prompt:**
```
Here are all the open loops from my business right now: [paste everything in your head]
Organize these into 4 categories:
1. Urgent & Important (do this week)
2. Important but not urgent (schedule)
3. Urgent but not important (delegate or batch)
4. Neither (eliminate or park)

Then suggest 3 "Big Three" priorities I should focus on this week to move the business forward most.
```

Pin the output. That's your north star for the week.

---

## Monday: CEO Review Day

**Theme:** See the full picture. Make the high-level decisions before the week pulls you into execution mode.

**Morning Block (60–90 minutes)**
- Review last week's metrics: revenue, leads, content performance, support tickets
- Check on any open decisions or commitments
- Confirm the week's Big Three priorities

**AI Prompt — Monday CEO Briefing:**
```
I'm doing my Monday CEO review. Here's my business data from last week:
- Revenue: [amount]
- New leads: [number]
- Content published: [list]
- Key wins: [list]
- Key problems or blockers: [list]

Act as my chief of staff. Give me:
1. A one-paragraph honest assessment of last week's performance
2. The one number I should be most focused on improving this week
3. Any decisions I'm avoiding that I need to make today
4. A suggested order for my Big Three priorities this week based on what you know
```

**Monday Rhythm:**
| Time | Task |
|---|---|
| 8:00 AM | Review metrics dashboard |
| 8:30 AM | Run CEO briefing prompt |
| 9:00 AM | Process inbox to zero (flag, respond, delegate) |
| 9:30 AM | Set weekly task list in project management tool |
| 10:00 AM | Deep work on Big Three Priority 1 |

**End-of-Day Check:** Did I make the 3 most important decisions I needed to make today? If not, why?

## Tuesday: Content Batch Day

**Theme:** Create everything for the week in one focused session. Never create content reactively.

Batching content creation is one of the highest-leverage shifts a solo founder can make. When you write one piece at a time, you context-switch constantly. When you batch, you get into a creative flow state and produce 3x more in the same time.

**Morning Block (2–3 hours)**
- Write or dictate raw ideas first — no editing yet
- Run AI enhancement prompts
- Schedule everything via Buffer, Hypefury, or Publer

**AI Prompt — Tuesday Content Batch:**
```
I'm batching content for this week. My business is [describe]. My audience is [describe].
This week's content theme: [theme]

Generate:
1. One LinkedIn post (insight-driven, 150–200 words, first-person, no hashtag spam)
2. One Twitter/X thread (8 tweets, first tweet is a hook, ends with a takeaway)
3. One email newsletter intro paragraph (100 words, conversational, one key insight)
4. One short-form video script (60 seconds, hook → problem → solution → CTA)

Tone: [your tone]. Brand voice: [describe your voice].
```

**Tuesday Rhythm:**
| Time | Task |
|---|---|
| 8:00 AM | Brain dump — all content ideas for the week |
| 8:30 AM | Run content batch prompt |
| 9:30 AM | Edit, add your voice, add real examples |
| 11:00 AM | Schedule all content |
| 11:30 AM | Deep work on Big Three Priority 2 |

**Pro Tip:** Record a 10-minute voice memo on your commute or morning walk. Transcribe it with Otter.ai and feed it to the content prompt. Your most authentic ideas come when you're not at a keyboard.

## Wednesday: Outreach and Revenue Day

**Theme:** Follow up on every open opportunity. Make the asks. Move money.

Most solo founders are afraid of Wednesdays. Sales feels like rejection. But Wednesday is the most important day of your week because this is where revenue is created. AI removes the friction from sales by doing the heavy lifting on copy and personalization.

**Morning Block (90 minutes)**
- Review your pipeline: who needs a follow-up?
- Research the top 3 prospects you want to reach this week
- Send 10–15 personalized outreach messages

**AI Prompt — Wednesday Pipeline Review:**
```
Here are my current open sales opportunities:
[paste deal name, stage, last contact date, and what's blocking each one]

Act as a sales coach. For each opportunity:
1. What is the most likely reason it has stalled?
2. What should my next message say to re-engage without sounding desperate?
3. Should I push harder, soften the ask, or let it go?

Then write the 3 highest-priority follow-up messages for today.
```

**Wednesday Rhythm:**
| Time | Task |
|---|---|
| 8:00 AM | Pipeline review |
| 8:30 AM | Run pipeline review prompt |
| 9:00 AM | Personalize and send follow-ups |
| 10:00 AM | Send 5–10 new outreach messages |
| 11:00 AM | Follow up on any proposals or quotes |
| 2:00 PM | Customer calls or demos |

---

## Thursday: Product and Operations Day

**Theme:** Build, improve, and systematize. This is where the actual product gets better.

Thursday is your "heads-down" day. No outbound sales. Minimize meetings. Focus on the work that builds long-term value: improving the product, documenting processes, building systems.

**Morning Block (2–3 hours of deep work)**
- Work on product or service improvements
- Write one SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) for a recurring task
- Identify one process you can automate or AI-delegate

**AI Prompt — Thursday Ops Builder:**
```
I run [describe business]. I'm spending [X hours per week] on [specific task].
Here's how I currently do it: [describe the process in detail]

Help me:
1. Identify which steps in this process could be handled by AI or automated
2. Write a simple SOP I could hand to a VA or future team member
3. Suggest one tool I might be missing that could cut this time by 50%
```

**Thursday Rhythm:**
| Time | Task |
|---|---|
| 8:00 AM | Deep work sprint 1 (no notifications) |
| 10:00 AM | Build or improve one product feature/service |
| 12:00 PM | Write one SOP using AI prompt |
| 1:30 PM | Review and respond to customer feedback |
| 3:00 PM | Process improvement task (automate one thing) |

---

## Friday: Review and Plan Day

**Theme:** Look back with honesty. Look forward with clarity. Set up Monday for success.

Friday done right means Monday starts running, not crawling. The weekly review is not optional — it's the engine that makes the whole system compound over time.

**The Weekly Review (45 minutes)**

**Step 1 — Capture:** What happened this week? List it out without judgment.

**Step 2 — Measure:** Did you hit your Big Three priorities? What got done? What didn't?

**Step 3 — Reflect (with AI):**
```
Here is my week in review: [paste your capture]
Big Three priorities this week were: [list]
What I actually completed: [list]
What I didn't complete and why: [list]

Give me:
1. An honest performance assessment — no sugarcoating
2. The pattern in what I'm avoiding or consistently not completing
3. What I should do differently next week based on this data
4. A suggested Big Three for next week
```

**Step 4 — Plan:** Set next week's Big Three. Block time for them on your calendar before you close the laptop.

**Friday Rhythm:**
| Time | Task |
|---|---|
| 8:00 AM | Clear all open tasks and inbox |
| 9:00 AM | Write weekly capture (what happened?) |
| 9:30 AM | Run Friday review prompt |
| 10:15 AM | Plan next week — set Big Three |
| 10:45 AM | Block calendar for next week's deep work |
| 11:00 AM | Log decision of the week |

---

## The Non-Negotiables

No system works if you don't protect the inputs. These three commitments make the weekly rhythm sustainable:

1. **Protect your morning block.** No meetings before 10 AM Monday–Thursday. This is when your best thinking happens.

2. **Single-task.** AI is there when you need it, but deep work requires sustained focus. Keep your phone in a drawer during work blocks.

3. **Don't skip Friday review.** When you skip the review, you lose the feedback loop. You repeat the same mistakes. The review is the whole point.
